## Decisions Made

- **Removed SettingsCentered:** The one-time-center-only behavior was a session-level optimization from Phase 8. With primary-monitor-aware positioning, always-centering is both cleaner and more correct for multi-monitor setups.
- **Monitor fallback chain:** `cursor_position` + `monitor_from_point` when `follow_cursor=true`, otherwise skip to `primary_monitor()` → `current_monitor()` → `center()`. This ensures the launcher always lands on a known monitor.
- **Settings window uses work_area math:** Instead of `win.center()` (which centers on whatever monitor the hidden window is on), the new code resolves `primary_monitor().work_area()` and computes the center position, guaranteeing the settings window appears on the primary display regardless of where it was last positioned.
